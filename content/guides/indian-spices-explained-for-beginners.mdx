Indian cooking relies on layering spices to build flavour. Once you understand a few key spices, you can cook a huge range of dishes.

This guide explains the most important spices beginners should understand first.

## The Core Curry Spices

These form the base flavour of many Indian dishes.

## Tempering Spices (Tadka Spices)

These spices are usually fried in hot oil at the start of cooking.

[CARD: Mustard seeds | /images/guides/indian-spices-explained-for-beginners/mustard-seeds.png | Nutty seeds that pop in hot oil and release flavour.]
[CARD: Cumin seeds | /images/guides/indian-spices-explained-for-beginners/cumin-seeds.png | Adds aroma and depth when fried.]
[CARD: Fenugreek | /images/guides/indian-spices-explained-for-beginners/fenugreek.png | Slight bitterness used in small amounts.]

## Supporting Flavour Spices

These help round out dishes and add complexity.

[CARD: Asafoetida | /images/guides/indian-spices-explained-for-beginners/asafoetida.png | Strong savoury spice used especially in dal and bean dishes.]
[CARD: Garam masala | /images/guides/indian-spices-explained-for-beginners/garam-masala.png | Used as a finishing spice rather than base spice.]

## How Spices Are Typically Used

Most Indian dishes follow a similar pattern:

1. Heat oil
2. Add whole spices
3. Add onion/garlic/ginger
4. Add powdered spices
5. Add main ingredients
6. Finish with garam masala or fresh herbs

Understanding this pattern makes most recipes easier to follow.

## Beginner Spice Shopping List

If you only buy a few spices, start with:

- turmeric powder
- cumin seeds
- coriander powder
- cumin powder
- chilli powder
- garam masala
- mustard seeds

With just these you can cook many Indian dishes.

## Storage Tips

- Store spices in airtight containers
- Keep away from sunlight
- Whole spices last longer than ground spices
- Replace ground spices every 6–12 months for best flavour

## Final Thoughts

Indian cooking becomes much easier once you recognise these core spices. Most recipes simply use different combinations of the same ingredients.

Once these spices become familiar, you can cook confidently without needing to follow recipes exactly.
