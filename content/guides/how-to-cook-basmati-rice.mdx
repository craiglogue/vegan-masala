Basmati rice is the most common rice served with Indian food. When cooked properly it should be light, fluffy, and the grains should stay separate rather than sticky.

This simple method works every time and is ideal for beginners.

## Ingredients

[CARD: Basmati rice | /images/guides/how-to-cook-basmati-rice/basmati-rice.png#center62 | Long grain rice known for its fragrance and fluffy texture.]
[CARD: Water | /images/guides/how-to-cook-basmati-rice/water.png#top30 | Needed to hydrate and cook the rice evenly.]
[CARD: Salt | /images/guides/how-to-cook-basmati-rice/salt.png#center60 | Enhances flavour and prevents bland rice.]
[CARD: Oil or vegan butter | /images/guides/how-to-cook-basmati-rice/oil-or-vegan-butter.png#center72 | Optional but helps keep grains separate and adds flavour.]

## Step-by-Step Method

1. Measure 1 cup basmati rice.
2. Rinse the rice in cold water 3–4 times until the water runs mostly clear.
3. Soak the rice for 15–20 minutes if you want an even fluffier result.
4. Bring 2 cups water to a boil in a saucepan.
5. Add salt and oil or vegan butter if using.
6. Add the rice and stir once gently.
7. Lower the heat, cover with a tight-fitting lid, and simmer for 10–12 minutes.
8. Turn off the heat and leave the rice covered for 5 minutes.
9. Fluff gently with a fork before serving.

## Key Tips for Perfect Rice

- Always rinse basmati rice to remove excess starch.
- Do not keep lifting the lid while it cooks.
- Let the rice rest before fluffing.
- Use a fork rather than a spoon to separate the grains.
- A little oil can help the grains stay separate.

## Common Mistakes

[CARD: Too much water | /images/guides/how-to-cook-basmati-rice/water.png#top30 | Too much water can make the rice soft, heavy, and sticky.]
[CARD: Not rinsing rice | /images/guides/how-to-cook-basmati-rice/not-rinsing-rice.png#center60 | Skipping rinsing leaves starch on the rice which causes clumping.]
[CARD: Cooking on high heat | /images/guides/how-to-cook-basmati-rice/cooking-on-high-heat.png#center55 | High heat causes uneven cooking and water evaporating too fast.]

## Optional Flavour Additions

You can make plain basmati rice more aromatic by adding:

- cumin seeds
- bay leaf
- cardamom pod
- cinnamon stick

These small additions turn plain rice into a simple fragrant rice that pairs beautifully with curries and dal.

## Serving Suggestions

Basmati rice pairs especially well with:

- dal
- chana masala
- vegetable curries
- tofu curry
- rajma

## Final Thoughts

Perfect basmati rice is mostly about technique rather than ingredients. Once you understand the rinse, simmer, and rest process, you will get consistent results every time.

This is one of the most useful basic skills in vegan Indian cooking.
