You do not need a huge kitchen or specialist equipment to cook great Indian food at home. A few reliable tools make the process easier and more consistent.

## A Good Pan or Kadai

A heavy-based frying pan, sauté pan, or kadai is ideal for most Indian curries and vegetable dishes.

It helps with:

- even cooking
- reducing sauces properly
- preventing spices from burning too quickly

## Saucepan or Pot

You will need a saucepan or medium pot for:

- dal
- rice
- soups
- one-pot curries

## Sharp Knife

Indian cooking often involves chopping onions, garlic, ginger, tomatoes, herbs, and vegetables, so a good sharp knife makes preparation much easier.

## Spice Jars

Keeping spices in clearly labelled jars helps speed up cooking and keeps your pantry organised.

## Blender or Small Grinder

A blender is useful for:

- curry bases
- chutneys
- spice pastes
- smooth sauces

## Rice Pot or Rice Cooker

A rice cooker is convenient, but a simple saucepan is perfectly fine if you know how to cook basmati rice well.

## Final Thoughts

The most important tools are the ones you use regularly. You do not need a complicated setup — just reliable basics that let you cook with confidence.
