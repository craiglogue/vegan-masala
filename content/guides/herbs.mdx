Fresh herbs play an important role in Indian cooking. While spices provide depth and warmth, herbs add freshness, colour, and contrast.

Many dishes use herbs either at the very end of cooking or as a garnish to lift the flavour of rich curries and dals.

## Coriander (Cilantro)

Coriander is probably the most recognisable herb used in Indian cooking.

[CARD: Fresh coriander | /images/guides/herbs.jpg | Usually chopped and added at the end of cooking or used as a garnish to bring freshness to rich curries and dals.]

It pairs well with:

- chickpea dishes  
- lentil dals  
- potato curries  
- tomato-based sauces  

If a dish tastes a little heavy, adding fresh coriander at the end can help balance the flavour.

## Mint

Mint is used more sparingly but adds a distinctive cooling flavour.

[CARD: Mint leaves | /images/guides/herbs.jpg | Fresh mint is often used in chutneys, rice dishes, and refreshing accompaniments.]

Common uses include:

- mint chutney  
- biryani and rice dishes  
- fresh salads  

Mint is especially useful when a meal contains spicy or rich foods.

## Curry Leaves

Despite the name, curry leaves do not taste like curry powder. They have their own unique citrusy, savoury flavour.

[CARD: Curry leaves | /images/guides/herbs.jpg | Usually tempered in hot oil at the start of cooking to release their aroma.]

They are especially common in:

- South Indian dals  
- coconut-based curries  
- vegetable stir-fry dishes  

Fresh curry leaves have a much stronger flavour than dried ones.

## Fenugreek Leaves (Methi)

Fenugreek leaves add a distinctive savoury aroma and slightly bitter edge.

[CARD: Fenugreek leaves (methi) | /images/guides/herbs.jpg | Often used fresh or dried in creamy curries and rich sauces.]

They are commonly used in dishes like:

- methi curries  
- creamy tomato-based sauces  
- restaurant-style gravies  

Even small amounts can change the flavour of a dish noticeably.

## Fresh vs Dried Herbs

Fresh herbs are most common in everyday cooking, but dried herbs are also used.

### Fresh herbs

- brighter flavour  
- used as garnish  
- added near the end of cooking  

### Dried herbs

- more concentrated flavour  
- added earlier in cooking  
- used in smaller quantities  

Fenugreek leaves are a good example of an herb that is often used dried.

## Final Thoughts

Herbs are often the finishing touch that brings a dish together. Even a simple curry or dal can feel more vibrant and balanced when fresh herbs are added at the end.

Keeping coriander, mint, and a few curry leaves in your kitchen can make a big difference to everyday vegan Indian cooking.
