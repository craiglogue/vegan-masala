Many Indian dishes use dairy for richness, tang, or texture, but these same effects can be achieved with good vegan alternatives.

This guide covers simple swaps that work well in everyday Indian cooking.

## Curd / Yoghurt

Unsweetened plant yoghurt works well in many Indian recipes where curd is used for tang and richness.

Best options:

- unsweetened coconut yoghurt
- unsweetened soya yoghurt

Use it in marinades, curry bases, and finishing sauces.

## Ghee

Ghee can be replaced with:

- vegan butter
- neutral oil
- coconut oil, depending on the dish

For everyday cooking, a neutral vegetable oil is usually enough.

## Cream

Creamy curries can be made with:

- oat cream
- coconut cream
- cashew cream

Choose based on the dish. Coconut cream works especially well in South Indian recipes, while oat or cashew cream gives a more neutral richness.

## Paneer

Paneer can often be replaced with firm tofu.

For the best results:

- press the tofu first
- use firm or extra-firm tofu
- add it later in the cooking process to stop it breaking up

## Milk

For sauces or drinks, use unsweetened plant milk such as:

- oat milk
- soya milk
- almond milk

## Final Thoughts

Good vegan Indian cooking is not about making dishes feel like substitutes. It is about using the right plant-based ingredients so the dish still feels rich, balanced, and satisfying.

Recipes where these swaps work well include:

- Palak Tofu
- Tofu Butter Masala
- Matar Tofu
- Madras Curry Tofu Casserole
