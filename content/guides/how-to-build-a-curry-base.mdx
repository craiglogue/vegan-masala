A good curry base is what makes Indian food taste rich, balanced, and properly layered.

Most curries are built in stages, and once you understand that process, many recipes begin to feel much easier.

## The Basic Curry Base

A simple curry base usually starts with these ingredients.

[CARD: Onions | /images/guides/placeholder.jpg | Often cooked first to create sweetness, body, and depth.]
[CARD: Garlic | /images/guides/placeholder.jpg | Adds savoury aroma and richness.]
[CARD: Ginger | /images/guides/placeholder.jpg | Brings warmth and freshness to the base.]
[CARD: Tomatoes | /images/guides/placeholder.jpg | Add acidity, body, and colour to many curry sauces.]
[CARD: Ground spices | /images/guides/placeholder.jpg | Provide warmth, depth, and character once added to the onion base.]
[CARD: Oil | /images/guides/placeholder.jpg | Carries flavour and helps the spices bloom properly.]

## The Typical Order

A simple curry base often follows this sequence:

1. Heat oil.
2. Add whole spices if using them.
3. Cook onions until softened or golden.
4. Add garlic and ginger.
5. Stir in ground spices.
6. Add tomatoes or puree.
7. Cook until thickened before adding the main ingredient.

## Why This Works

Each stage builds flavour in a different way:

- onions add sweetness
- aromatics add freshness and depth
- spices bring warmth
- tomatoes balance richness with acidity

## Common Mistakes

- adding spices too early and burning them
- not cooking onions long enough
- adding too much water too soon
- not seasoning enough at the end

## Final Thoughts

Once you understand the structure of a curry base, many Indian recipes stop feeling complicated. The ingredients may change, but the core technique is often very similar.
