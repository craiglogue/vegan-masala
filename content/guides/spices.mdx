Indian cooking relies on a small number of core spices used in different combinations. Once you understand what each spice does, it becomes much easier to cook flavourful curries, dals, and rice dishes at home.

## The Core Spices to Start With

## Whole Spices

Whole spices are usually heated in oil first to release their aroma and flavour. This step is especially important in many curries, dals, and rice dishes.

[CARD: Fennel seeds | /images/guides/spices.jpg | Slightly sweet and aromatic, often used in richer curries.]
[CARD: Fenugreek seeds | /images/guides/spices.jpg | Bold and slightly bitter, best used in small amounts for depth.]

## What Each Spice Does

Different spices play different roles in Indian cooking.

### For warmth and depth

- cumin
- coriander
- cinnamon
- cloves

### For heat and colour

- chilli powder
- Kashmiri chilli powder
- black pepper

### For aroma

- garam masala
- cardamom
- bay leaves

### For sharpness or tang

- mustard seeds
- amchur powder
- chaat masala

Understanding these roles makes it easier to improvise and balance flavours.

## What to Buy First

If you are starting from scratch, buy these first:

- turmeric powder
- cumin seeds
- coriander powder
- cumin powder
- chilli powder
- garam masala

Once you are comfortable with those, add:

- mustard seeds
- Kashmiri chilli powder
- cardamom
- fenugreek
- asafoetida

## Final Thoughts

You do not need a huge spice cupboard to cook good Indian food. Starting with a few core ingredients and learning how they work together will take you a long way.

Once you understand these spices, recipes like Chana Masala, Bombay Aloo, Mushroom Masala, Vegetable Vindaloo, and many dals become much easier to cook with confidence.
