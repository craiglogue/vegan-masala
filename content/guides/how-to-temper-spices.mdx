Tempering spices, often called tadka, is one of the most important techniques in Indian cooking. It involves frying whole spices in hot oil so they release their aroma and flavour into the dish.

## What You Usually Temper

These are some of the most common ingredients used in tadka.

## Why Tadka Matters

Tempering is not just decorative. It changes the flavour of the whole dish by releasing the essential oils in the spices.

A well-made tadka can make a simple dal or vegetable dish taste much more rounded and aromatic.

## Basic Method

A simple tempering method usually looks like this:

1. Heat a small amount of oil in a pan.
2. Add whole spices such as cumin seeds or mustard seeds.
3. Let them sizzle and release their aroma.
4. Add aromatics like dried chillies, curry leaves, or garlic if needed.
5. Either continue cooking the dish in that pan or pour the tempered oil over the finished dish.

## When to Use Tadka

Tempering can be used at different stages depending on the recipe.

### At the beginning

This builds the base flavour of the dish.

### At the end

This is often poured over dal just before serving to add aroma and visual appeal.

## Tips for Better Tempering

- Have all your ingredients ready before heating the oil.
- Do not let the spices burn.
- Use medium heat rather than very high heat.
- Work quickly once the spices hit the pan.

## Final Thoughts

Tempering spices is a small technique that makes a big difference. Once you get used to it, your dals, curries, and vegetable dishes will taste far more balanced and fragrant.
