## Ingredients
- cumin seeds
- coriander seeds
- fennel seeds
- fenugreek seeds
- green cardamom pods
- cinnamon stick
- cooked chickpeas
- coconut oil
- curry leaves
- onion
- ginger garlic paste
- tomatoes
- turmeric
- paprika
- cayenne
- water or vegetable stock
- coconut milk
- cilantro
- salt

## Method
1. Blend the spices in a grinder or blender until fine, then set aside.
2. In a sauté pan, heat the oil and add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, mix in the onions, curry leaves, and ginger garlic paste with salt, cooking until the onions are translucent.
3. Incorporate the tomatoes, paprika, cayenne, turmeric, and the spice mix. Cook on medium heat, stirring frequently until the tomatoes darken and most liquid evaporates.
4. Stir in the chickpeas and additional salt, then add water or stock. Bring to a boil, reduce the heat, and simmer for 10 minutes.
5. Mix in the coconut milk and heat until warm.
6. Taste and adjust salt as necessary. Garnish with chopped cilantro and serve hot.

## Notes
- You can use any neutral oil instead of coconut oil if preferred.
- For added nutritional value, consider throwing in some spinach or kale during the simmering phase.
- Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat gently on the stovetop.
