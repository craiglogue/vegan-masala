## Ingredients
- dried chickpeas, soaked overnight
- neutral oil
- cumin seeds
- ajwain (optional)
- bay leaf
- cinnamon stick
- black cardamom pods
- cloves
- finely chopped onion
- chopped ginger
- chopped garlic
- green chillies, slit
- chopped tomatoes
- turmeric
- Kashmiri chilli powder
- salt
- water
- garam masala
- amchur (or lemon juice)
- fresh coriander to garnish

## Method
1. Drain soaked chickpeas and rinse well.
2. Turn Instant Pot to sauté mode and heat oil.
3. Add cumin, ajwain, bay leaf, cinnamon, cardamom and cloves and cook until fragrant.
4. Add onion and sauté until softened.
5. Add ginger, garlic and green chillies and cook briefly.
6. Add tomatoes and cook until soft and pulpy.
7. Stir in turmeric and chilli powder.
8. Add chickpeas, salt and water and scrape the bottom of the pot.
9. Pressure cook on high for 30 minutes, then natural release for 12–15 minutes.
10. Switch to sauté mode and simmer to thicken.
11. Mash a few chickpeas to thicken the gravy.
12. Stir in garam masala and amchur (or lemon juice).
13. Garnish with coriander and serve.

## Notes
- Adjust the level of green chillies for spice preference.
- This dish can be stored in the fridge for up to 3 days.
